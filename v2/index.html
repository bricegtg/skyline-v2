The Skyline method

Nobody should die cleaning a window. And nobody should pay three weeks of scaffolding to do it.

For a century, cleaning the untouchable meant a person on a rope, a swing stage in the wind, an insurance file nobody wanted to open. We rebuilt the whole job around a machine that holds station to two centimetres, sprays purified water at calibrated pressure, and lands when the crew says so. The operator stays on the ground. The drone does the height.

How it works

From brief to final flight report in five steps.

  1. 01
    Site brief

    You send us a building photo, the floor count, and what's not working. We respond within 24 hours with a proposal range and call.

  2. 02
    Aerial survey

    Free 30-minute drone walk-around. We capture facade condition, identify hazards, and flag CAAP airspace constraints before quoting.

  3. 03
    Fixed-scope quote

    Per-floor or per-panel pricing. No hidden lift mobilisation, no scaffold day-rate. What you sign is what we bill.

  4. 04
    Flight day

    Crew of three on site: lead pilot, support pilot, ground safety. Operation runs in 60-minute flight blocks until the surface is signed off.

  5. 05
    Flight report

    You receive a delivery PDF: before/after photography, area covered, water/chemical usage, flight logs, and recommended next interval.

Method · The four-part system

It's not a hose with rotors. It's a precision system.

01
Purified water

Reverse-osmosis on-site. Zero mineral spotting — no rinse pass needed, no streaking.

02
Calibrated pressure

30–60 PSI through a soft-jet nozzle. Safe for glass, sealants, stone, and solar.

03
RTK station-hold

2 cm horizontal accuracy. The drone tracks a vertical line without drift, even in gusts.

04
Full flight log

Telemetry, photo, and area-coverage data captured automatically. Delivered as a PDF.
