The case for the IND-160-F

The fire is won or lost in the first six minutes.

A ladder truck takes time to arrive, time to set up, and tops out around the seventh floor. The IND-160-F is airborne in six minutes and reaches 160 metres — putting water or foam on a high-rise, a refinery tank, or a wildland front while the first crew is still mobilising. It is not a replacement for firefighters. It is the asset they wish they had before the ladder was even raised. And for the first time, it is something a Philippine fleet can actually own.

Where it deploys

One platform. Six fire grounds.

01

High-rise

Tower and mall fires above ladder reach. Puts foam on a 40th-floor face while crews stage the interior attack.

02

Refinery

Tank, depot, and tanker fires. Approaches heat and toxic plume that ground crews can't, foam-coating from above.

03

Industrial

Warehouse and factory blazes. Thermal mapping finds the seat of the fire before water is ever committed.

04

Wildland

Forest and grass fronts. Repeated 500L drops along a containment line, day or night, without crewed aircraft risk.

05

Stadium

Arena roofs and large-span venues. Reaches truss-bay fires that no aerial appliance can physically access.

06

Settlement

Dense informal settlements. Aerial water where trucks can't enter the alleys — slowing spread between structures.
