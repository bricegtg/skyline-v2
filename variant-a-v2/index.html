01 Chapter One
The Premise

A building is a problem of access, not a problem of height.

For four decades, the only way to wash a 42-storey tower was to send a man over the edge. Scaffolding for a month. Bosun's chairs in the wind. Liability insurance that priced out half the country's portfolio.

We removed the man. The platform stayed where it belonged — on the ground. The water — and the work — went up.

It is not a faster method. It is a different method. A tower we used to schedule by season, we now schedule by weekday.

03 · Method

From brief to handover, in five moves.

No surprises mid-job. Each phase has a deliverable, a timestamp, and a name attached to it.

STEP 01 · DAY 0

Brief

Inside 24 hours, a director-level call. We agree the surface, the constraint, the window.

STEP 02 · DAY 1–3

Survey

Drone photogrammetry of the facade. We model the surface and price against it — not an estimate.

STEP 03 · DAY 4–5

Mobilise

Permits, NOTAMs, building management protocol. We handle the paper so you don't.

STEP 04 · WINDOW

Execute

One platform crew on the ground. Two drones in rotation. Pre/post photogrammetry as proof.

STEP 05 · DAY +1

Handover

Inspection report. Insurance certificates. Before/after package signed off by a named director.
