The Premise

A building is a problem
of access, not a problem
of height.

The ladder, the swing-stage, the rope-access team — all artefacts of a moment when getting to height was the hard part. That moment ended.

We operate two payload classes of unmanned aircraft up to 160 metres. One carries 500 litres of cleaning solvent at calibrated pressure. The other carries a 500-litre tank of firefighting agent and a thermal-guided lance. Both reach the 40th floor in under six minutes from dispatch. Neither requires a single worker to leave the ground.

What this changes, in practical terms: the insurance, the schedule, the regulatory exposure, the public-perception risk, and — for the building owners we work with — the question of whether the job gets done at all this quarter.
