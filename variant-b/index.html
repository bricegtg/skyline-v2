Chapter 01 · The Manifesto

FOR EVERY SURFACE.
FOR EVERY EMERGENCY.

Why we built
Skyline Drones.

The Philippine skyline is changing — taller, more complex, more exposed. The people who maintain it, and the responders called when it burns, are still operating with rope, scaffolding, and luck.

Skyline Drones was founded to change that posture. We deploy autonomous drone fleets across two divisions — exterior building cleaning and aerial firefighting support — to remove human risk from the most dangerous work in the built environment.

"The entirety of your building — and the moments that test it — maintained to the same standard. Without a single worker leaving the ground."

Across glass curtain walls, natural stone, metal cladding, terracotta, concrete and aluminium composite, we deliver a faster, safer, and more cost-effective alternative to traditional rope-access and scaffolding methods. And when a fire breaks the skyline, our aerial reconnaissance and thermal mapping fleet partners with fire services to deliver situational awareness no ladder can reach.

Chapter 05 · The Process

FROM BRIEF TO COMPLETION
IN WEEKS, NOT MONTHS.

Step 01

Site assessment & quotation

We review facade specs, materials and access needs — and return with a precise scope, cost comparison vs. your current method, and a no-obligation engagement letter.

Step 02

Safety briefing & CAAP approval

We secure airspace clearance, coordinate ground perimeters, and run a documented pre-flight safety briefing with building management and tenants.

Step 03

Drone deployment

Setup in 30–60 minutes. No scaffolding, no cranes, no boom lifts. Drone, water source, and ground station — no more.

Step 04

Autonomous cleaning flight

Pre-programmed flight paths, monitored live by licensed RPIC pilots. Every surface — glass, cladding, concrete, stone, metal — at any height up to 160 metres.

Step 05

Final inspection report

Photographic before/after, surface condition notes, and a full inspection report delivered the same day. No follow-up calls required.
