Frequently Asked

What we
get asked.

Q.01 How fast can you mobilise? +
Inside Metro Manila and our Gulf hub radii, our SLA is six minutes from dispatch to first-water for firefighting and 24-hour survey-to-quote for cleaning. Outside that radius, mobilisation is scoped per engagement.
Q.02 Are you certified and insured? +
Yes. All operations are conducted under CAAP certification with NOTAM filings for every flight. We carry USD 5M per-incident insurance and provide certificates of insurance ahead of mobilisation.
Q.03 Do you operate outside the Philippines? +
We hold an active Gulf desk in Riyadh and a partner cell in Doha for KSA, UAE, and Qatar engagements. Programmes outside that footprint are scoped on request — typically as a forward operating base agreement.
Q.04 Do you replace traditional fire response? +
No — we complement it. Skyline operates under BFP-allied dispatch protocols and co-deploys with municipal engines and ladder companies. We reach what they cannot. They handle ground and rescue.
Q.05 What does a retainer cost? +
Cleaning retainers begin at PHP 120,000 per quarter. Firefighting retainers begin at PHP 250,000 per quarter and scale with the asset's hazard profile, occupancy, and required SLA. Master programmes and industrial responses are scoped by RFP.
Q.06 Can the rig operate at night? +
Yes. Night-VFR operations are part of our standard certification, with thermal targeting and floodlight coverage from the rig itself. Many cleaning sorties are scheduled overnight to minimise tenant disruption.
