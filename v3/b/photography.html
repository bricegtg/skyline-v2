Why drone-captured

Altitude is an angle no crane can reach.

The decisive frame almost always sits between thirty and one hundred and sixty metres — too high for a jib, too low and too expensive for a helicopter, and impossible from the street. That gap is exactly where a drone lives, and it is where the establishing shot, the context, and the scale of a project finally make sense.

We fly that altitude every working day. Every shoot runs under CAAP airspace clearance and full third-party liability cover, with a certified Remote Pilot in Command on the sticks and a documented flight plan filed before the first rotor turns. The result is footage that looks effortless and a paper trail that satisfies the most cautious legal and insurance review.

  • Altitudes from 30 to 160 metres — the angle a crane or jib physically cannot hold
  • 4K / 60fps capture on a stabilised gimbal, graded to your brand palette
  • CAAP airspace clearance and full insurance filed before every flight
  • Same-day proxy delivery; final grade and reel within the agreed window
