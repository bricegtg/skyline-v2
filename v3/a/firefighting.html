The case for the IND-160-F

The fire is won or lost in the first six minutes.

A ladder truck takes time to arrive, set up, and tops out around the seventh floor. The IND-160-F is airborne in six minutes and reaches 160 metres — putting water or foam on a high-rise, a refinery tank, or a wildland front while the first crew is still mobilising. And for the first time, a Philippine fleet can own it.

Where it deploys

One platform. Six fire grounds.

01High-riseTower and mall fires above ladder reach. Puts foam on a 40th-floor face while crews stage the interior attack.
02RefineryTank, depot, and tanker fires. Approaches heat and toxic plume ground crews can't, foam-coating from above.
03IndustrialWarehouse and factory blazes. Thermal mapping finds the seat of the fire before water is ever committed.
04WildlandForest and grass fronts. Repeated 500L drops along a containment line, day or night, without crewed-aircraft risk.
05StadiumArena roofs and large-span venues. Reaches truss-bay fires no aerial appliance can physically access.
06SettlementDense informal settlements. Aerial water where trucks can't enter the alleys — slowing spread between structures.
